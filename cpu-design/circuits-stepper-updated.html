CIRCUITS: Stepper

A guided walkthrough of the stepper circuit used to sequence CPU control signals.

The Stepper Circuit

The "Stepper" allows us to cycle through a set/enable sequence for writing to and reading from the bus. Based on our design, we repeatedly cycle through six distinct steps. The basic unit is two S–R latches (1‑bit memory) in series. This allows the output of the second latch to be 1 when the value set in the first latch is 1 and the set for the second latch is 1; otherwise, the second latch outputs 0. Cascading these units passes a 1 down the chain step‑by‑step, energizing outputs in sequence.

Two 1‑bit CPU memory circuits (S–R latches) in series form the base of our stepper. Chaining many of these together yields a single high output at a time, as demonstrated in the videos below.
