Part 1: Boolean Algebra Basics

At its core, Boolean algebra deals with just two values: TRUE (1) and FALSE (0). [cite_start]There are three fundamental operations[cite: 1]:

  • AND (¬∑): The output is TRUE only if all inputs are TRUE. Think of it as "A and B".
  • OR (+): The output is TRUE if at least one input is TRUE. Think of it as "A or B (or both)".
  • NOT ('): The output is the opposite of the input. It inverts the value.
