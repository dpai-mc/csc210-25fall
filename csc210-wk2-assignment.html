Boolean Expression Notation Guide

Understanding Boolean Symbols:

  • AND Operation: AB or A·B (multiplication)
  • OR Operation: A + B (addition)
  • NOT Operation: A' or A (negation)

Critical: Order of Operations

In expressions like A'B, the NOT symbol applies only to the variable it immediately follows. This means:

A'B = (NOT A) AND B
AB' = A AND (NOT B)
A'B' = (NOT A) AND (NOT B)

Always read the NOT symbol as applying to the single variable directly before it, just like a mathematical exponent.

Part C: De Morgan's Laws 40 Points

▼

In this section, you will prove one of De Morgan's laws by building and comparing two different circuits in a single subcircuit.

Subcircuit Name: demorgan-proof

Within this single subcircuit, build two smaller circuits that share the same two inputs (A and B):

  1. Circuit 1: Implement the expression (AB)'. This is a NAND gate. The output should be labeled out-nand.
  2. Circuit 2: Implement the expression A' + B'. This is (NOT A) OR (NOT B). The output should be labeled out-or-nots.

Toggle the inputs A and B to show that for every combination, out-nand and out-or-nots are always identical, thus proving the law.

Tips for Success

▼

Label Everything

Use labels for all your inputs and outputs in Logisim. This makes your circuits much easier to read and debug.

Test Incrementally

After you place a few gates, test them with the poke tool (the hand icon). Don't wait until the entire circuit is built to find a mistake.

Stay Organized

Keep your wires clean and untangled. Logisim automatically routes wires, but you can drag them to make your layouts neater.

Save Often

Save your .circ file frequently to avoid losing your work.
